to Industry Evolution

Understanding why organizations succeed is only half the story. Understanding why industries fail reveals the same operational principles from an entirely different perspective.

FM: The Fading Modulation of Terrestrial Radio

For nearly a century, terrestrial radio informed, entertained, connected communities, and introduced audiences to new voices and new music. It became one of America's most influential communication systems.

The Telecommunications Act of 1996 fundamentally changed that system. Consolidation, automation, economics, technology, and changing audience habits reshaped an industry that once depended on local ownership, live personalities, and community engagement.

This book examines those changes through the lens of operational systems rather than nostalgia. It explores how incentives, technology, leadership, and economics combined to reshape one of America's defining media platforms.

The lessons extend well beyond broadcasting. Every complex system eventually faces pressure to automate, centralize, reduce costs, and operate with fewer people. Understanding radio's evolution provides valuable insight into those broader operational challenges.
