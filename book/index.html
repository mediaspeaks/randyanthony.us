Reliability Is Not Improvised

How Systems Drift, Recover, and Behave Under Real-Time Pressure

Every real-time system eventually reveals its true behavior. Reliability Is Not Improvised explores how seemingly insignificant operational deviations accumulate into significant disruptions through the author's original Reaction Cascade Effect framework.

Drawing upon decades of broadcast and operational experience, Randy Anthony explains why reliability is not a feature that can be installed, but a discipline that must be practiced, protected, and continually improved.

FM: The Fading Modulation of Terrestrial Radio

How the Telecommunications Act of 1996 Changed Radio, Consolidated Stations, and Transformed the Listening Experience

FM traces the evolution of one of America's most influential communications industries—from its golden age through deregulation, consolidation, automation, and today's digital challenges.

More than a history book, FM examines the operational decisions, leadership choices, and business philosophies that reshaped an industry once built upon localism, creativity, and community.
