A Career Focused on Complex Systems

Throughout four decades in radio, television, automation, operational monitoring, engineering support, and time-critical decision making, one idea has remained remarkably consistent.

Complex systems succeed only when people can understand them, observe them, and respond before small failures become major ones.

These books explore that philosophy from two different perspectives.

Reliability Is Not Improvised

Reliable organizations rarely become reliable by accident. They are intentionally designed, intentionally observed, intentionally maintained, and intentionally improved.

Drawing on decades inside live television, broadcast automation, operational monitoring, control rooms, and high-pressure environments, this book introduces a practical framework for operational reliability that applies far beyond broadcasting.

Rather than focusing on technology alone, it explores visibility, decision quality, dependencies, operational drift, escalation, resilience, and the human factors that determine whether complex systems continue functioning under stress.

FM: The Fading Modulation of Terrestrial Radio

Radio once connected communities, introduced new artists, informed local audiences, and reflected regional culture. It wasn't simply entertainment. It was infrastructure.

Through deregulation, consolidation, automation, changing economics, and evolving technology, that infrastructure gradually changed into something very different.

This book examines how those changes occurred, why they mattered, and what every modern industry can learn from radio's transformation.

While written for radio professionals and enthusiasts, the lessons extend far beyond broadcasting. Every complex system eventually faces the same pressures: efficiency, automation, consolidation, and the challenge of remaining valuable while technology continues to evolve.
