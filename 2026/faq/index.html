What is the Piety Token & Pay-chain initiative? ⌄
It’s a utility-focused digital asset concept designed to function inside a real-world economic system—supporting payroll flows, debit card usage, bill payments, vendor settlement, and related participation experiences.
What blockchain standard is used? ⌄
The token is described as an ERC-20 digital asset on Ethereum, making it portable across compatible wallets and verifiable on-chain.
What is the total supply? ⌄
The project describes a hard-capped, minted supply of 3,100,000,000 tokens, with a limited float intended for early phase visibility and discovery.
What is the core utility? ⌄
The stated primary utility is to power an engine that includes:
  • Payroll activity (native demand tied to payroll flows)
  • Debit card activity
  • Bill pay
  • Vendor settlement
  • Participation experiences inside the broader ecosystem
Why emphasize “utility” versus speculation? ⌄
The project messaging emphasizes real-world usage and a closed-loop design—meaning value is intended to be driven by economic throughput (e.g., payroll conversion and settlement) rather than hype cycles.
Will the token always be broadly available on public markets? ⌄
The public-market phase is described as an early visibility / verification period. Over time, the intent is for most transactional volume to route through a private exchange environment to support payroll, debit cards, and bill pay operations. Availability can depend on liquidity, demand, and regulatory considerations.

The token is described as a utility token within a private membership ecosystem. Access may be restricted in certain jurisdictions. It is each participant’s responsibility to comply with applicable local laws before acquiring or using any tokens or services.

Digital assets involve substantial risk, including possible loss of all value.
