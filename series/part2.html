Topic Rotation — Part II

Part II builds on Part I by focusing on visibility in time-critical systems.

Reliability depends not only on design and process, but on the ability to observe system behavior in real time.

This phase examines how information moves through operational systems:

  • What signals are visible to operators
  • What information is delayed or obscured
  • How monitoring systems interpret events
  • Where alerting creates clarity or confusion
  • How decision-making is shaped by available data

The focus shifts from structure to perception — from how systems are built to how they are understood under pressure.

In time-critical environments, what is not visible often becomes the source of disruption.
