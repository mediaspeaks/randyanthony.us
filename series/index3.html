Operational Architecture Series

Operational Architecture

A complete twenty-seven-part exploration of operational reliability, operational visibility, and the Reaction Cascade Effect.

Beginning with the foundational principles of reliable systems and culminating in operational visibility, this series examines how complex systems behave, adapt, recover, and support real-time decision-making under pressure.

Although inspired by decades of broadcast and streaming operations, the concepts presented throughout this series apply equally to manufacturing, healthcare, transportation, utilities, finance, public safety, and every organization where operational continuity matters.

Part I — Foundations

Part I establishes the operational foundation of the series through fourteen articles examining reliability, invisible work, dependencies, interfaces, redundancy, automation, accountability, prevention, and operational architecture.

These principles form the framework upon which the remainder of the Operational Architecture Series is built.

Begin Part I →

Part II — Operational Visibility

Building upon the operational foundations established in Part I, Operational Visibility introduces the Reaction Cascade Effect while exploring monitoring, awareness, escalation, decision-making, recovery, and system behavior under pressure.

The second half of the series shifts from how reliable systems are built to how they are observed, interpreted, and managed in real time.
