About the Series

The Operational Architecture Series began as a thirteen-week examination of the principles that support reliable operations in broadcast, streaming, and other time-critical environments.

A second thirteen-week volume expanded those foundations through Operational Visibility, examining how information is created, interpreted, delayed, distorted, and ultimately influences decision-making under operational pressure.

Together these twenty-seven essays form a practical body of work connecting operational reliability, human performance, organizational design, and resilient system architecture.

The series also serves as the conceptual foundation supporting the ideas developed throughout Reliability Is Not Improvised and the broader Operational Reliability Advisory practice.

Beyond the Series

The weekly Operational Architecture Series concludes with Part II, but the work continues.

Operational reliability is never static. Every new technology, workflow, and organizational change creates new operational challenges requiring greater visibility, clearer decision-making, and stronger system design.

Future writing, published books, consulting, and speaking engagements will continue expanding these principles across industries where timing, coordination, and resilience remain mission critical.

The goal has always been larger than a weekly series—it has been to build a practical body of knowledge that helps organizations anticipate failure, improve visibility, and strengthen operational performance.

Continue the Journey

The Operational Architecture Series concludes its twenty-seven-week exploration of operational reliability and operational visibility, but the ideas continue well beyond these pages.

Reliability Is Not Improvised expands upon the principles introduced throughout the series, including the original Reaction Cascade Effect framework and the practical realities of managing complex, time-critical systems.

FM: The Fading Modulation of Terrestrial Radio examines how leadership, technology, regulation, and operational decisions reshaped an entire industry—offering lessons that extend far beyond broadcasting.

Whether your responsibility is keeping operations running, leading organizational change, or simply understanding how reliable systems succeed under pressure, these books provide a deeper exploration of the principles introduced throughout this series.
