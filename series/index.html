Operational Architecture Series

Operational Architecture

A complete twenty-seven-part exploration of operational reliability, operational visibility, and the Reaction Cascade Effect.

Beginning with the foundational principles of reliable systems and culminating in Operational Visibility, this series examines how complex systems behave, adapt, recover, and support real-time decision-making under pressure.

Although inspired by decades of broadcast and streaming operations, the principles throughout this series apply equally to manufacturing, healthcare, transportation, utilities, logistics, finance, public safety, and every organization where operational continuity matters.

Begin Part I Begin Part II

Part I — Foundations

The first fourteen articles establish the operational principles that support dependable systems. Topics include reliability, invisible work, dependencies, interfaces, redundancy, automation, accountability, prevention, and operational architecture.

Rather than focusing on specific technologies, Part I examines the disciplines that enable complex operational environments to remain stable, resilient, and predictable when systems are placed under real-world pressure.

Together these articles create the operational framework that every concept explored in Part II builds upon, providing the foundation for understanding visibility, system behavior, and the Reaction Cascade Effect.

Begin Part I →

Part II — Operational Visibility

Part II expands those operational foundations through visibility, monitoring, decision-making, escalation, recovery, and the introduction of the Reaction Cascade Effect.

Building upon the principles established in Part I, this section explores how operators interpret information, recognize developing conditions, prioritize competing signals, and respond effectively as complex systems evolve under pressure.

The emphasis shifts from how reliable systems are built to how they are observed, understood, and managed in real time, where operational visibility becomes the foundation for confident decision-making and resilient performance.

Begin Part II →

Reliability Is Not Improvised

The Operational Architecture Series introduced the principles. Reliability Is Not Improvised brings those principles together into a single volume, expanding the discussion of operational reliability, operational visibility, resilience, and the complete Reaction Cascade Effect into one comprehensive reference.

Written for operational leaders, engineers, broadcast professionals, managers, and anyone responsible for mission-critical systems, the book extends the series with additional real-world examples, operational models, and practical applications.

About Randy Anthony

Randy Anthony is the creator of the Operational Architecture Series and author of Reliability Is Not Improvised. Drawing upon more than four decades of operational experience in broadcast, streaming, automation, workflow design, and master control, his work focuses on operational reliability, systems thinking, operational visibility, and decision-making within time-critical environments.

His writing, research, and advisory work are dedicated to helping organizations better understand how complex operational systems behave before, during, and after disruption.
