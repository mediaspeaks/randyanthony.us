About the Series

The Operational Architecture Series began as a structured exploration of the principles that govern reliable operations across broadcast, streaming, and other mission-critical environments.

Part I established thirteen foundational principles that influence operational performance, while Part II expanded those concepts through Operational Visibility, examining how information, timing, and human awareness shape resilient systems.

Together, these twenty-seven weekly essays form a comprehensive body of work that complements the ideas presented throughout Reliability Is Not Improvised while standing on their own as a practical resource for operational leaders.

Beyond the Series

Although the weekly Operational Architecture Series concludes with Part II, the work itself continues.

The principles introduced throughout the series continue through published books, operational advisory services, future articles, and speaking engagements focused on operational reliability, leadership, and resilient system design.

Reliable organizations are never finished. They continually improve their visibility, their decision-making, and their ability to perform under pressure.

Continue the Journey

The Operational Architecture Series concludes its twenty-seven-week exploration of Operational Reliability and Operational Visibility, but the principles continue well beyond these pages.

Reliability Is Not Improvised expands upon the concepts introduced throughout the series, including the Reaction Cascade Effect and the practical discipline required to build resilient, mission-critical operations.

FM: The Fading Modulation of Terrestrial Radio examines how leadership, technology, regulation, and operational decisions reshaped an entire industry while offering lessons applicable to organizations far beyond broadcasting.

Whether you are responsible for critical operations, leading organizational change, or simply interested in how reliable systems succeed under pressure, these books provide a deeper exploration of the ideas introduced throughout the Operational Architecture Series.
