About the Operational Architecture Series

The Operational Architecture Series is a complete twenty-seven-part study of how complex systems behave when reliability, timing, visibility, and decision-making intersect. Rather than focusing on specific technologies, these articles examine the operational principles that determine whether systems remain stable or begin to fail under pressure.

Part I — Foundations establishes the core disciplines of operational reliability through fourteen articles covering reliability, invisible work, composure, dependencies, interfaces, redundancy, automation, accountability, prevention, and system architecture.

Part II — Operational Visibility builds upon those foundations by introducing the Reaction Cascade Effect and examining how operational awareness, signal prioritization, monitoring, recovery, decision-making, and visibility determine how organizations respond as systems begin to drift.

Although inspired by decades of experience in broadcast and streaming operations, the concepts presented throughout this series apply equally to manufacturing, transportation, healthcare, logistics, utilities, finance, emergency management, and every environment where operational continuity depends upon disciplined execution and informed decisions.

About the Author

Randy Anthony

Randy Anthony is the creator of the Operational Architecture Series and author of Reliability Is Not Improvised.

Drawing upon more than four decades of operational experience in broadcast, streaming, automation, workflow design, and master control, his work focuses on operational reliability, system architecture, resilience, visibility, and decision-making within time-critical environments.

Through writing, advisory services, and original research, Randy helps organizations better understand how complex operational systems behave before, during, and after disruption.
