About the Series

The Operational Architecture Series was written to examine how complex operational systems behave when timing, coordination, and reliability matter most. While examples are drawn from broadcast and streaming operations, the principles extend to any environment where systems, people, technology, and decisions must work together under real-time pressure.

Part I establishes the operational foundation by exploring the disciplines that create dependable systems—from reliability, interfaces, redundancy, automation, and accountability to the hidden work that keeps organizations functioning every day.

Part II builds upon those foundations by introducing the Reaction Cascade Effect, examining how visibility shapes operational awareness, influences decisions, and determines whether small disruptions remain isolated or develop into larger cascading failures.

Together, these twenty-seven articles form a single body of work intended for operational leaders, engineers, managers, business continuity professionals, and anyone responsible for designing, operating, or improving mission-critical systems.

Part I — Foundations

The first fourteen articles establish the principles that support reliable operational systems. Topics include reliability, invisible work, dependencies, interfaces, redundancy, automation, accountability, prevention, and the architectural thinking required to build resilient organizations.

Part I provides the operational framework upon which the remainder of the series is built.

About Randy Anthony

Randy Anthony is the creator of the Operational Architecture Series, author of Reliability Is Not Improvised and FM: The Fading Modulation of Terrestrial Radio, and founder of Operational Reliability Advisory.

His work draws upon more than four decades of experience in broadcast operations, live production, master control, workflow design, automation, and operational leadership.

Through publications, advisory services, and original research, he focuses on operational resilience, visibility, systems thinking, and helping organizations understand how complex systems behave under real-time pressure.
