About the Operational Architecture Series

The Operational Architecture Series examines how complex systems behave when reliability, visibility, timing, and human decision-making intersect. Rather than focusing on specific technologies, these articles explore the operational principles that determine whether systems remain stable—or begin to drift under pressure.

Part I — Foundations introduces fourteen operational disciplines including reliability, invisible work, dependencies, interfaces, redundancy, automation, accountability, prevention, and architecture.

Part II — Operational Visibility builds upon those foundations through thirteen additional articles while introducing the Reaction Cascade Effect, examining how visibility shapes operational awareness, influences decisions, and determines whether isolated problems remain contained or expand across interconnected systems.

Part I

Fourteen articles introducing the principles that support reliable operational systems. Topics include reliability, invisible work, dependencies, interfaces, redundancy, automation, accountability, prevention, and operational architecture.

Every concept introduced later in the series builds upon these foundational operational disciplines.
